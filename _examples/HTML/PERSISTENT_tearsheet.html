The signal

Priced for deceleration — not the streak, not a collapse

Persistent has compounded USD revenue at ~17% in FY26 and ~24% over five years, with no down quarter in 24. The market, at its own ~10% discount rate, prices roughly 8% perpetual FCF growth. The shaded band is the gap the thesis must close — the bear says it widens to zero as AI compresses effort; the bull says remediation demand keeps the line in the mid-teens.

Where the contested base sits

The deflation leg touches a slice; the durable block is regulated and complex

AI effort-compression bites hardest in commoditized coding / ADM / testing — a sub-set of the Technology/TMT book and parts of BFSI. The durable, harder-to-automate block is the multi-phase, regulated Healthcare & BFSI programs and the data-foundation / modernization work that is the remediation opportunity. Geography is the real fragility: North America is ~81% of revenue.

The dossier · evidence behind the numbers

What's underneath each weight

The door: AI deflation vs. remediation demand

P1a / R5 · −14% vs +14%

The bear is real and measured: GitClear's 211M-line study shows AI-era code churn rising from 3.1% to 5.7% and refactoring collapsing from 25% to under 10%; Harness finds most developers now spend more time debugging AI output; ~40% of AI code in security-sensitive contexts carries critical vulnerabilities; METR (2025) found experienced devs slower with AI on mature codebases. The consensus has translated this into ~2–3%/yr services deflation, already in FY27–28 estimates. But the same forces are the reward: MIT NANDA finds 95% of enterprise GenAI pilots produce no P&L impact, with ~80% of pilot-to-production work being data engineering, governance and integration — exactly what Persistent sells, and exactly the "data foundations built ahead of demand" management points to. Net at our scoping: the two cancel. That is the honest statement of the bet.

Nagarro: scale bought at a steep premium, on borrowed money

P4 / R3 · the day's −11%

Persistent is acquiring Nagarro (Munich; ~€1bn revenue) at an EV of €1.27bn, €81/share — a ~140% premium to the undisturbed price, though only ~9.1× EV/EBITDA. Funded by a €1.4bn Barclays bridge (~4.5%, 18-month) with a €1.54bn corporate guarantee — no equity dilution, but it ends the debt-free balance sheet that was a pillar of the quality premium. Rough math: ~€140M EBITDA (~14%, below Persistent's ~20%), ~€63M/yr interest, giving an estimated ~₹265 Cr PAT contribution (~+14%) — mildly accretive on base, consistent with management's "cash-EPS accretive Year 1," but dilutive if the lower-margin ERP/SAP mix or integration slips. The reward: Europe rises from 9% to 22% of revenue, four of Europe's top-five automakers, and a $2.9bn combined platform. The market's −11% prices the near-worst case.

Margin floor & the FY27 reset

P2 / P6 · −6.6% / −3.0%

FY26 EBIT margin was 15.6% — structurally below TCS/Infosys (~20–25%) and a real ceiling on intrinsic value. The $2bn revenue run-rate slipped to Q4 FY27; CLSA (high-conviction outperform, PT ₹6,520) and Nomura (neutral, PT ₹5,200) cut FY27–28 EPS by 2–7% on the margin miss. Persistent must also fund its own AI delivery layer (SASVA, harnesses, inference) — the cost side of the complexity-tax that you flagged the market is under-weighting in one direction and over-fearing in the other.
