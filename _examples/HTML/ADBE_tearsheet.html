The freemium ↔ lock-in reckoning

PENALTY · retention −4%

The FTC/DOJ case settled for $150M ($75M civil penalty + $75M free services) under ROSCA, approved by Judge Noël Wise on 10 Apr 2026. The injunction forces clear early-termination-fee disclosure, trial-conversion reminders, and frictionless cancellation. President David Wadhwani was named personally. With subscriptions at 97% of revenue, the read is that some headline retention was cancellation friction, not loyalty — now legally removed. True voluntary stickiness is untested, and the freemium pivot is partly a forced migration to funnel-and-value retention. This is the origin of the "ARR durability" fear.

The moat, precisely — what it does and doesn't protect

REWARD · lock-in +7%

~98% of the Fortune 500, 30M+ subscriptions, ~130% net revenue retention, file-format standards (.PSD/.AI/.PDF), and 3-year enterprise ETLAs. Acrobat/PDF is the most durable layer. The nuance that makes this leg both the dominant reward and the mirror of the dominant penalty: lock-in protects the installed base, not new-creator entry. The wall is high around who's already inside; it does nothing for who walks in next.

"Design is dying" is the wrong question

CONTEXT · entry leak

BLS projects a ~2–3% decline in traditional graphic-design employment through 2032 — but UX/product design +16%, and the total design market grows from ~$46B to ~$78–81B. The field bifurcates: commodity execution collapses, strategic / brand / UX / motion expands. Adobe runs 850M+ MAUs (+17%). The key distinction: a labor trend is not a software-spend trend — spend rises as non-designers buy tools. So the entry leak is a share / monetization question, not a terminal-decline input.

The AI pivot is an aggregation play, not a model race

REWARD · monetization +4%

Generative credits = tokens (Adobe's own framing — "like a data plan"); premium and video models cost more, and no-rollover drives consumption. Adobe opened the model layer — 30+ third-party models including Veo, OpenAI, Runway, Luma, Kling, FLUX and Topaz — while keeping orchestration, billing, and commercial-safety (Content Credentials) closed. That commoditizes supply and owns demand: Adobe needn't win the model race to win the workflow. Topaz was acquired to bring the best primitive in-house. AI-first ARR tripled past $500M.

Management is variance, not direction

PENALTY · execution −4%

Narayen (18 years, ~25× revenue) steps down once a successor is named and stays as Chair. CFO Dan Durn left for Marvell on 15 Jun — a simultaneous CEO+CFO vacancy during a risky pivot. Internal contenders: Wadhwani (named in the DOJ complaint) and Chakravarthy; an external AI-focused search is underway. Model this as a wider distribution, not a direction. Critically, the thesis doesn't need Adobe to become customer-friendly — the predatory, high-switching-cost engine is load-bearing and profitable. The CEO question barely moves intrinsic value.

Delta · since the working doc

What's new — and what it does to the thesis

Topaz Labs — confirmed. The acquisition the doc assumed is now announced (~24 Jun, terms undisclosed), validating the aggregation thesis — best upscaling primitive brought in-house.

Semrush — acquired. Bolsters total ARR to ~$27.1B (+12.5%), but means part of the growth is inorganic. The thesis lives on organic new-creator entry, so this partially masks the very signal you need to see. → new monitorable: organic vs. inorganic ARR.

Buyback got more accretive. At the $247 authorization price it was ~23% of cap; at ~$195 it's now ~32%. The de-rating mechanically boosted the per-share value transfer.

Analyst capitulation is now visible, not just 13F-implied: RBC $350→$285, Citi $264→$228, Freedom Broker cut to Hold; mean PT compressed to ~$282. The Street's specific fear is exactly the big penalty leg — does the funnel convert.

Agentic positioning. Cannes Lions partnerships (Accenture, Omnicom, WPP, Anthropic, Microsoft) and Disney tapping Adobe for concept design — small "door stays open" tells reframing Adobe as agentic infrastructure rather than disruption target.
